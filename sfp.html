Corelink SFP Pon Module

SFP PON (Small Form-factor Pluggable Passive Optical Network) modules play a crucial role in the evolution of fiber optic networks, enabling high-speed data transmission over optical fibers. These compact modules are designed to fit into SFP ports on networking devices such as switches, routers, and optical line terminals (OLTs), facilitating the deployment and scalability of fiber optic infrastructure.

Compact Form Factor:

SFP PON modules are small and hot-pluggable, allowing for easy installation and replacement without disrupting network operations. Their compact size makes them ideal for space-constrained environments.

High-Speed Connectivity:

With support for Gigabit and beyond, SFP PON modules deliver high-speed data transmission, meeting the increasing bandwidth demands of modern applications such as streaming video, cloud computing, and IoT

Interoperability:

SFP PON modules adhere to industry standards, ensuring compatibility with a wide range of networking equipment from different vendors. This interoperability promotes flexibility and vendor neutrality in network design and deployment.



Basic Parameter

Feature Description
Wavelength SFP PON modules typically operate at different wavelengths, such as 1490nm downstream and 1310nm upstream in GPON systems, or 1490nm downstream and 1550nm upstream in EPON systems. These wavelengths are standardized for compatibility with PON networks.
Reach The reach of an SFP PON module refers to the maximum distance over which it can transmit optical signals reliably. This distance can vary depending on factors such as the quality of the optical fiber, signal attenuation, and network configuration. Typical reaches range from a few kilometers to tens of kilometers.
Split Ratio The split ratio determines the number of subscribers served by a single optical line terminal (OLT) port. For example, a split ratio of 1:32 means that one OLT port can serve up to 32 subscribers. SFP PON modules support various split ratios to accommodate different deployment scenarios and subscriber densities.
Encryption Some SFP PON modules support encryption features to enhance network security and protect subscriber data. Encryption mechanisms such as AES (Advanced Encryption Standard) are used to encrypt traffic between the OLT and the optical network units (ONUs) or optical network terminals (ONTs), ensuring confidentiality and integrity.
Environmental Conditions SFP PON modules are designed to operate in a wide range of environmental conditions, including temperature, humidity, and altitude. They are built to withstand harsh outdoor environments and are often deployed in outdoor cabinets or enclosures. Temperature ranges typically extend from -40°C to 85°C.
