Corelink Power Meter

An optical power meter (OPM) is a type of electronic test device used to measure the power output of fiber optic equipment or the power or loss of an optical signal transmitted through a fiber cable. An OPM uses a photodiode to generate an electrical current proportional to optical power.

Basic Optical Power Meters:

Compact fiber power meters with outsized capabilities have revolutionized the fiber testing landscape. The MP-60/-80 Miniature USB 2.0 Power Meters exemplify this trend by putting power and loss testing into the palm of your hand

PON Optical Power Meters:

The SmartPocket series also includes the dedicated OLP-37 PON power meter for downstream power testing of B-PON, E or G-PON, and 10G-EPON or XGS-PON networks. The SmartClass Fiber OLP-87 PON power meter is an advanced, wavelength selective power meter capable of simultaneous upstream and downstream power measurement over live PON networks. The OLP-87 is compatible with the same B, E, G-PON, and high-speed PON networks using 10G-EPON, XGS-PON, or NG-PON2 technologies.



Basic Parameter

Wavelength Range Specifies the range of wavelengths the power meter can measure.
Power Measurement Range Indicates the range of power levels the meter can accurately measure.
Accuracy Defines the accuracy of power measurements provided by the meter.
Resolution Refers to the smallest increment of power that the meter can detect.
Calibration Details whether the meter requires periodic calibration for accurate readings.
Interface Specifies the type of interface used for connecting the power meter to other devices.
Data Logging Indicates whether the meter can log power measurements over time.
Display Describes the type of display (e.g., LCD) and its features (e.g., backlight).
Power Source Specifies the power source required to operate the meter (e.g., battery, AC adapter).
Size and Weight Provides dimensions and weight of the meter for portability and installation purposes.
