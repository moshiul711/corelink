Corelink G-EPON MINI OLT

The Corelink G-EPON MINI OLT is a network device used in fiber-optic communication networks. OLT stands for Optical Line Terminal, which is a device located at the service provider's central office that connects the customer's premises to the wider network.

Port Density:

Despite being "MINI," the OLT likely offers a considerable number of ports for connecting to subscriber optical network terminals (ONTs). These ports may be in the form of Gigabit Ethernet or 10-Gigabit Ethernet interfaces

Management Interface:

It likely provides a web-based graphical user interface (GUI) or a command-line interface (CLI) for network management tasks such as configuration, monitoring, and troubleshooting.

Reliability and Redundancy:

It's common for OLTs to incorporate redundancy features such as dual power supplies and hot-swappable components to minimize downtime in case of hardware failures.
