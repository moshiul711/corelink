Corelink W-70 Splicer Machine

The Corelink W-70 Splicer machine is a piece of equipment used in the manufacturing industry, particularly in the field of paper converting and web processing. It's primarily designed for splicing or joining two rolls of material together seamlessly, typically used in scenarios where continuous production is required, such as in printing, packaging, or paper converting operations.

Splicing Mechanism:

This is the core component responsible for joining two rolls of material together. It usually involves precise cutting and bonding techniques to ensure a seamless transition between rolls without interrupting the production process.

Splice Detection System:

Some advanced splicers may include sensors or cameras to detect splice quality, ensuring that any faulty splices are identified and corrected before they can cause issues downstream in the production process.

Material Alignment System:

Ensures that the rolls of material are properly aligned before splicing, minimizing waste and ensuring accurate positioning of the splice.
